&#39;Times New Roman&#39;, serif;font-size: 16px;line-height: 125%;text-align: left;\">Covering Penn State presents a unique challenge.<br/>\n<br/>\nNo, it’s not having to sit through every Penn State fan’s take on why Michigan sucks.<br/>\n<br/>\nThe challenge has to do with access — specifically, access to open records.<br/>\n<br/>\nTaxpayer-funded organizations in the U.S. — from school districts to borough councils to local police — are largely subject to open records laws. In Pennsylvania, <a href=\"https://www.spotlightpa.org/news/2022/03/right-to-know-pa-panel-transparency/\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">these statutes are known as “Right-to-Know laws.”</a><br/>\n<br/>\nIf you’re curious about <a href=\"https://www.spotlightpa.org/news/2021/07/contact-tracing-data-breach-pennsylvania-emergency-contracts/\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">which companies bid for public contracts and how much your local government paid</a>, you can file a Right-to-Know request. <br/>\n<br/>\nWant to know <a href=\"https://www.spotlightpa.org/news/2020/01/right-to-know-missing-records/\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">whether law enforcement is discriminating when pulling people over for traffic stops</a>? File a Right-to-Know request.<br/>\n<br/>\nBut if you want to know what’s happening in Old Main, the Right-to-Know Law offers little, if any, assistance. <br/>\n<br/>\nPenn State is one of four “state-related” institutions in Pennsylvania. The others are University of Pittsburgh, Lincoln University, and Temple University. The special designation means Penn State is not subject to open records laws beyond the public disclosure of basic financial information.<br/>\n<br/>\nSo, our success covering Penn State — and the public’s ability to understand what is going on inside a nearly $8 billion institution with tremendous influence in the state — relies on you.<br/>\n<br/>\nMany of the best investigative stories are possible because of people inside an institution who know what is happening and want to solve a problem or put an end to waste, fraud, or abuse.
