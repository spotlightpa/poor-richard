125%;text-align: left;\"><strong>On March 20, 2019, a State College police officer shot and killed Osaze Osagie,</strong> <strong>a 29-year-old Black man experiencing a mental health crisis.</strong><br/>\n<br/>\nThe shooting marked the first time in State College Police Department history that an officer killed someone. The community responded with outrage, sparking a series of follow-up actions by the State College Borough Council.<br/>\n<br/>\nOne was to establish the Community Oversight Board, a group of civilians tasked with oversight of the police. That board was empowered to create a <a href=\"https://content.civicplus.com/api/assets/pa-statecollege/55d8a2e2-f931-4c92-b830-95e695be3194/information-on-filing-with-cob-91622-1page.pdf?sq=808a942d-b2e1-d88c-23f4-4f31c780acc8&amp;scope=all\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">new civilian complaint process</a>, through which it could “receive, process, and investigate complaints about the (police department) from members of the community.”<br/>\n<br/>\nOn Nov. 10, the board approved that <a href=\"https://content.civicplus.com/api/assets/pa-statecollege/a42947eb-4ac6-42f0-96ba-85085642ea5e/9-8-22-draft-civcompprocess.pdf?sq=7b270b14-f1ae-7c67-dfca-a02b79d8c91c&amp;scope=all\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">process</a>, and it is expected to be open to the public in January. Here’s how it will work:<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>What information is collected about the person submitting the complaint?</strong><br/>\n<br/>\nPeople submitting complaints have the option to submit them anonymously, though they are asked to provide contact information either way. Third parties can submit complaints on behalf of someone else.<br/>\n<br/>\nThe <a href=\"https://content.civicplus.com/api/assets/pa-statecollege/cd51d2f1-d3e6-4d44-8129-e708ae52c89e/submission-complaint-form-91622.pdf?sq=44f093b1-f1fd-1103-8593-7ebd69c5e1e1&amp;scope=all\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">Community Oversight Board Complaint Form</a> also asks for the gender and racial/ethnic background of the person submitting the complaint, but this information is not required.<br/>\n<br/>\nThe form asks the complainant to provide the location, date, and time of the incident; the name of the police officer or department employee, their badge number, a description of the officer/employee, and their gender and racial/ethnic background.<br/>\n<br/>\nIn addition to a complete description of the incident, complainants can also provide names and contact information for witnesses, and any available audio, video, or photos of the incident.</p>\n\n<p dir=\"ltr\" style=\"margin: 10px 0;padding: 0;mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #3b3b3b;font-family: Georgia, Times, &#39;Times New Roman&#39;, serif;font-size: 16px;line-height: 125%;text-align: left;\"><strong id=\"docs-internal-guid-a92ec9bb-7fff-a8c1-369e-5575ee7125ec\">How are complaints submitted?</strong></p>\nAssuming the civilian complaint process goes live in January as scheduled, people will be able to file a complaint: \n\n<ul>\n\t<li style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;\">Online</li>\n\t<li style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;\">By mailing the form to the Community Oversight Board, 243 S. Allen St., State College, PA 16801</li>\n\t<li style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;\">By faxing the form to 814-231-3082</li>\n\t<li style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;\">By submitting the printed form in person to the borough’s equity and inclusion director at the State College Municipal Building (address above)</li>\n\t<li style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;\">By emailing the form to <a href=\"mailto:ccp@statecollegepa.us\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">ccp@statecollegepa.us</a></li>\n\t<li style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;\">By calling 814-234-7100 (8 a.m. to 4 p.m. on weekdays) or 814-234-7150 (after hours, weekends, and holidays; this is the police department’s non-emergency number) — and asking for the director of equity and inclusion.</li>\n</ul>\n\n<p dir=\"ltr\" style=\"margin: 10px 0;padding: 0;mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #3b3b3b;font-family: Georgia, Times, &#39;Times New Roman&#39;, serif;font-size: 16px;line-height: 125%;text-align: left;\"><strong id=\"docs-internal-guid-deda7367-7fff-a271-5234-04fcddcd3faf\">What authority does the Community Oversight Board have to respond to complaints?</strong><br/>\n<br/>\n“We’re not going to be able to investigate,” Cynthia Young, the chair of the Community Oversight Board, told Spotlight PA. “And we’re not going to be able to make the State College PD do anything.”<br/>\n<br/>\nAccording to the complaint process, the Community Oversight Board “will not make determinations of SCPD employee misconduct.”<br/>\n<br/>\nThough the ordinance did empower the board to “investigate” complaints, the term was not defined and the board has chosen not to interpret it on its own.<br/>\n<br/>\nThe complaint process is “an alternate pathway to file a complaint or to voice concern” about police department employees, according to the board. Furthermore, “the priority of the CCP is to provide support to the complainant.”<br/>\n<br/>\nAll complaints submitted to the board are confidential regardless of whether someone provides their name<br/>\n<br/>\nReports involving “child abuse, physical harm or the threat of physical harm” must be forwarded to the State College Police Department.
