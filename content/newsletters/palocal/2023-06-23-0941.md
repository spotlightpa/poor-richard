He acknowledged no constituent approached him about the flag before his redesign resolution went public, though they definitely have since. (His name has already been added to the state flag’s dedicated <a href=\"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flag_of_Pennsylvania\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">Wikipedia page</a>, so no backing out now.) <br/>\n<br/>\nBut Webster seemed to genuinely believe that changing the flag could be transformative, and not just in the obvious way. That’s when he expects the floodgates to open. <br/>\n<br/>\nTwitter already has lots of comical ideas. flag is the embodiment of the place it represents, so criticizing the flag can be perceived as criticizing the place itself, thus any change may be resisted. And perhaps some people resist ANY change as a challenge to the status quo with which they are comfortable (and some use the language of ‘cancel culture’ in their emotional response).” <br/>\n<br/>\nUtah knows what he’s talking about.
