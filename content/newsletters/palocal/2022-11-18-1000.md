      <p dir=\"ltr\" style=\"margin: 10px 0;padding: 0;mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #3b3b3b;font-family: &#39;Open Sans&#39;, &#39;Helvetica Neue&#39;, Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif;font-size: 16px;line-height: 125%;text-align: left;\">Artist Jim Bachor is turning potholes across America into actual street art. And, yes, that includes Pennsylvania — <em><a href=\"https://www.mytwintiers.com/news-cat/regional-news/report-new-york-pennsylvania-among-10-worst-states-for-potholes/\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">naturally</a></em>.<br/>\n<br/>\nBachor, a Chicagoan and former advertising worker who&#39;s been dubbed &#34;Pothole Picasso&#34; by the New York Post, has done three installations in Philadelphia (<a href=\"https://www.bachor.com/artic-splash\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">see</a> <a href=\"https://www.bachor.com/nestle-crunch-bar-\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">them</a> <a href=\"https://www.bachor.com/make-your-mark-c17qy\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">here</a>). One earned him an unsolicited tip from a grateful neighbor. Another has since disappeared.<br/>\n<br/>\nThe process is fairly simple if not entirely legal: Bachor picks a pothole, fills it with concrete, and tops it off with mosaic tiles carefully arranged into the image of a <a href=\"https://www.bachor.com/copy-of-chicken-map\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">person</a>, <a href=\"https://www.bachor.com/copy-of-kenneth-ainslie-map\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">animal</a>, <a href=\"https://www.bachor.com/copy-of-pb-j\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">food</a>, <a href=\"https://www.bachor.com/copy-of-toilet-paper-holy-trinity\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">cleaning product</a>, or a <a href=\"https://www.bachor.com/copy-of-liar-2-1\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">statement</a>.<br/>\n<br/>\nWe talked to him about the work, the &#34;universal hate&#34; that potholes inspire, and when he&#39;s coming back to the craterlands of the Keystone State.<br/>\n<br/>\n<em>The conversation has been edited for clarity and length</em>.<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>PA Local: </strong>Hey Jim. Describe your ideal pothole.<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>Bachor: </strong>An ideal pothole is like 18 by 24 inches. Can&#39;t be too shallow. Can&#39;t be in the center of the road. I can&#39;t be blocking traffic. I can&#39;t be drawing too much attention to myself. I like to kind of get in and get out.<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>PA Local: </strong>Ever get in any trouble for this? <img align=\"right\" data-file-id=\"1957895\" height=\"284\" src=\"https://mcusercontent.com/77370ff1d001f9bb991fed9e7/_compresseds/be12c528-d09b-9d40-32f4-8082461c8adb.jpg\" style=\"border: 0px;width: 200px;height: 284px;margin: 15px 0px 15px 15px;outline: none;text-decoration: none;-ms-interpolation-mode: bicubic;\" width=\"200\"/><br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>Bachor:</strong> I&#39;ve done this almost 10 years and I&#39;ve had about eight encounters with the cops. My favorite happened at Hyde Park in Chicago. A cop car pulled up and I look up and he looks down at me and goes, &#39;Are you that pothole guy?&#39; I said yeah, and he goes, &#39;That&#39;s so f---ing cool.&#39;<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>PA Local: </strong>Pennsylvania has a proud history of <a href=\"https://www.mytwintiers.com/news-cat/local-news/residents-in-pa-town-plant-christmas-tree-in-a-pothole/\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">guerrilla pothole ac</a><a href=\"https://www.mytwintiers.com/news-cat/local-news/residents-in-pa-town-plant-christmas-tree-in-a-pothole/\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">tion</a>. How do non-police respond to your art? <br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>Bachor: </strong>The only time I&#39;ve ever had some blowback is if the piece is political. But it&#39;s almost always positive. A woman in Philadelphia actually gave me a little tip. She came out and I think she gave me $10. I never ask for that type of stuff, but she insisted and I thought that was kind of cute. Some people see it as kind of a Robin Hood thing. <br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>PA Local: </strong>Is it? Are these critiques of bad government services?<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>Bachor: </strong>People like to think that that my work is some statement about the lack of the government, you know, taking care of things. It really isn&#39;t that. It isn&#39;t trying to draw attention to the problem of potholes at all.<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>PA Local: </strong>What started it then? <br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>Bachor: </strong>It really started to kind of like get some self-promotion and get people to go to my website and look at <a href=\"https://www.bachor.com/originals-for-sale\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">my </a><a href=\"https://www.bachor.com/originals-for-sale\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">fine art</a>. <br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>PA Local: </strong>Everyone hates potholes. Setting aside the tangled knot of public opinion around infrastructure and how it&#39;s maintained (<a href=\"https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2018-06-14/why-domino-s-pizza-is-fixing-potholes-now\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">Hi, Domino&#39;s</a>), how does that popular sentiment influence what you do? <br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>Bachor: </strong>I kind of realized that potholes are a universal hate. It doesn&#39;t matter who you are; everyone hates them. And so I will sometimes juxtapose this universal hate with subject matter that is kind of universally loved, like flowers, or ice cream, or junk food. That kind of stuff. So there&#39;s that nice kind of tension between the two ideas.<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>PA Local: </strong>How are you picking your designs? <br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>Bachor: </strong>Sometimes I&#39;ll steer campaigns to the town I&#39;m in. Like, if I&#39;m in the middle of a junk food series and I&#39;m in Detroit — I&#39;m originally from Detroit— but if I&#39;m in Detroit I&#39;ll consider what junk food items are specific to it, like <a href=\"https://detroithistorical.org/learn/encyclopedia-of-detroit/faygo-pop\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">Faygo pop</a>. The Philly piece was that <a href=\"https://billypenn.com/2022/05/13/arctic-splash-ending-cartons-fishtown-iced-tea/\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">Arctic Splash</a> or whatever that iced tea thing is that you guys drink out there.<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>PA Local:</strong> How do you decide where you&#39;re going next? <br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>Bachor: </strong>I&#39;m not independently wealthy. So it&#39;s all based on people bringing me out. The Philly pieces were sponsored by a gallery in town, Paradigm. I did a bunch in Detroit because I was invited to a street fest there. I did some in Nashville because I was hired to do some promotional stuff for a country singer. So if I&#39;m somewhere for a reason, I&#39;ll bring a piece.<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>PA Local: </strong>How long do they typically last?<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>Bachor: </strong>If the pothole gods are looking down on me favorably, probably a couple of years. I&#39;ve had them last over five years, and they&#39;re in great shape. But I&#39;ve had other situations where it was gone in a week because the street got repaved and it&#39;s like you gotta be kidding me. <br/>\n<br/>\n<em>—<a
