style=\"margin: 10px 0;padding: 0;mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #3b3b3b;font-family: &#39;Open Sans&#39;, &#39;Helvetica Neue&#39;, Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif;font-size: 16px;line-height: 125%;text-align: left;\">National Pennsylvania Day (July 20) is just around the corner. But is it real?<br/>\n<br/>\nFor those unfamiliar, the holiday marking Pennsylvania’s entry into the Union, which actually <a href=\"https://www.abc27.com/digital-originals/is-july-20th-really-national-pennsylvania-day/\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">happened in December</a>, was created in a lab by a man, <a href=\"https://marloanderson.com/\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">Marlo Anderson</a>, through his North Dakota-based calendar company. <br/>\n<br/>\nIt’s been called an astroturfed, <a href=\"https://billypenn.com/2018/07/20/happy-national-pennsylvania-day-which-is-extremely-fake/\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">“extremely fake” holiday</a>, which definitely tracks. <br/>\n<br/>\nPennsylvania knows a thing or two about fakery, from <a href=\"https://www.cracked.com/article_28014_the-crazy-story-fake-burger-king-in-pittsburgh.html\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">fake Burger Kings</a> to fake <a href=\"https://twitter.com/ndf_phl/status/1673130440771731456\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">religious shrines</a> to <a href=\"https://www.ydr.com/story/opinion/columnists/2017/09/26/whats-up-cell-tower-looks-like-tree-rail-trail-column/703243001/\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">fake trees</a> and <a href=\"https://www.gq.com/story/the-great-high-school-impostor\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">fake children</a>. (Capital-Star has a list of <a href=\"https://www.penncapital-star.com/working-the-economy/its-pennsylvania-day-what-it-is-how-to-celebrate-it/\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">real ways to celebrate</a>.)<br/>\n<br/>\nBut Anderson insists the holiday isn’t “fake” so much as concocted out of thin air, and even if it is “fake,” he says, who cares? People enjoy it. And there’s a million hyperniche, made-to-order holidays out there already, many marketing gimmicks that stimulate the human want for occasion — and food and beverage sellers’ <a href=\"https://www.theatlantic.com/health/archive/2019/08/happy-national-corporate-promotional-day/596443/\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">want for more food and beverage buyers</a>. <br/>\n<br/>\nPA Local caught up with Anderson by phone to discuss National Pennsylvania Day, how many holidays is too many, this <a href=\"https://nationaldaycalendar.com/national-day-calendar-exposed-for-running-the-worlds-longest-running-april-fools-joke/\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">April Fool’s Eve press release</a> declaring it all a prank, the steady deluge of X-rated requests received by his office, and more.<br/>\n<br/>\n<em>Our conversation has been edited for clarity and length.</em><br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>PA Local:</strong> Let’s start with the March 31 press release that called all of this the “world’s longest-running April Fool’s joke.” Say it ain’t so. <br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>Anderson:</strong> Oh, that was just a joke. The stating of the fact that it’s the longest April Fool’s joke was an April Fool’s joke. <br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>PA Local:</strong> That’s so meta it’s making my head hurt.<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>Anderson:</strong> We like to have a little fun from time to time. A few years ago we did one saying Congress was debating adding 15 more days to the calendar. <br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>PA Local:</strong> But your business is real?<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>Anderson:</strong> Yes. It started out as a <a href=\"https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=E6T4opNu9ZY\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">blog about 10 years ago</a> and today we have a staff of about 12 [not including contractors] and receive 30,000 applications a year for new national holidays. <br/>\n<br/>\n<em>[Editor’s note: Anderson said they only charge for the few dozen they approve a year. Corporate packages for accepted submissions can run from <a href=\"https://nationaldaycalendar.com/corporate-packages/\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">$25,000 to $100,000</a>, and Anderson said they’re losing money on most deals. National Pennsylvania Day was not commissioned. Anderson’s company came up with the idea as a way to engage all 50 states.] </em><br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>PA Local:</strong> Could we apply, for example, to make June 23 National <a href=\"https://twitter.com/FarFarrAway/status/1672268438117535746\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">I-95 Reopening</a> Day? Would you accept that application? <br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>Anderson:</strong> It’s a pretty tough litmus test to become a new national holiday. And rightfully so. I mean, you can’t just declare them for all the things we have coming in or it just doesn’t mean anything anymore.<br/>\n<br/>\n<em>[Editor&#39;s note: See <a href=\"https://nationaldaycalendar.com/national-tweed-day-april-3/\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">National Tweed Day</a> and <a href=\"https://nationaldaycalendar.com/national-lima-bean-respect-day-april-20/#:~:text=NATIONAL%20LIMA%20BEAN%20RESPECT%20DAY%20%E2%80%93%20April%2020\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">Lima Bean Respect Day</a> on 4/20.]</em><br/>\n<br/>\nWe get questioned a lot about, you know, is there enough room in the calendar. Right now we have about 1,500 holidays. I figure we have room for maybe another 200 to 300 before it starts getting pretty crowded. You might have National Vegetarian Day on the same day as National Hamburger Day. We’re at the point where we’re thinking about how we can retire some.<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>PA Local:</strong> So is that a no on National I-95 Reopening Day?<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>Anderson:</strong> The first test is how people will look at this 30 or 50 years from now and the reopening of an interstate won’t pass that litmus test at all.<br/>\n<br/>\nWe had a lot of people wanting National COVID Day or National Pandemic Day and those types of things, but it’s like the swine flu of 1919, nobody talks about it anymore. Yet Oreo cookies are still talked about and they were around in 1919.<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>PA Local: </strong>A million Americans died from COVID. Surely that’s a lasting impact.<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>Anderson:</strong> Yeah, I mean, you know, I could see something like [National COVID Day] happening, but it would have to be presented in a way that I think would be clever, enduring, those types of things. Then, of course, yeah.<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>PA Local:</strong> But does that sort of prove the point that [the cottage calendar/made-to-order holiday industry] is more about commercialization than genuine commemoration? I realize every holiday is commercialized now, but usually that isn’t the initial reason for them.<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>Anderson:</strong> There’s certainly a commercial aspect to this. We get lobbied by companies like crazy. But you could make the same argument, like you said, about Easter and Christmas. And outside of Independence Day, every holiday has been made up. Somebody had an idea to make this or that a holiday and it was made a holiday because of that.<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>PA Local:</strong> What’s the weirdest pitch you’ve received?<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>Anderson:</strong> Well, if we ever wanted to start an adults-only calendar we could. You cannot imagine the requests we get. Sometimes we’ll get one: “You gotta declare National Heather Day,” and we’ll go through the whole process and then six weeks later we’ll reach out and say something like “I’m sorry we don’t do requests for individual people” and the person who submitted it will say “That’s OK, we broke up already.”<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>PA Local:</strong> What’s a weird pitch that’s been approved?<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>Anderson:</strong> National Sneak Some Zucchini Onto Your Neighbor&#39;s Porch Day (Aug. 8). But I don’t actually know if that was approved by us or if it was there before we started. <br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>PA Local:</strong> Anything else in the works?<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>Anderson:</strong> We’re working on a radio show and a <a href=\"https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tYWo4AJi6Gg\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">TV show</a>, and there’s a movie called <em>Oh, What a Day</em>. It’s set to start filming in upstate New York and Las Vegas this fall. It’s about a journalist named Claire Day, and she gets caught up in having to write about all the national holidays and experiencing them herself.<br/>\n<br/>\n<em>[Editor’s Note: Anderson said plans for a very similar sounding movie starring SNL’s Kate McKinnon — <a href=\"https://billypenn.com/2018/07/20/happy-national-pennsylvania-day-which-is-extremely-fake/\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">teased in this Billy Penn piece from 2018</a> — fell apart. He declined to discuss it in further detail on the record. McKinnon’s agent did not respond to PA Local by press time.]</em><br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>PA Local:</strong> OK, but setting aside the spinoffs, do we really need this many holidays? Why is there such a public appetite for this?<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>Anderson:</strong> I think people are looking for a bit of good news in this world and a reason to get together. I truly believe that’s what this is all about. <br/>\n<br/>\n—<em><a href=\"mailto:cdeppen@spotlightpa.org?subject=PA%20Local%20feedback\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">Colin Deppen</a>, Spotlight PA</em></p>\n\n
