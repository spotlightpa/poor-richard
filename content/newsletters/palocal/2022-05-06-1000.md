The plan: steal and gut a driverless car in a city whose climate and tech sector are putting the squeeze on workers. <br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>PA Local:</strong> Thanks. Are you comfortable with the sci-fi label? <br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>McGinty:</strong> I don’t personally view it that way. I kept trying to write it contemporaneously but things just kept changing in the driverless car sector and I finally just kind of threw up my hands and decided to throw the proverbial ball ahead thinking ‘they can’t catch me out there.’ <br/>\n<br/>\nAnd once you’re in the future you can have some fun with it. But if people read it and they’re like this is sci-fi, that’s great. It doesn’t bother me. <br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>PA Local: </strong>How’s the autonomous vehicle space race going in 2030 Pittsburgh [which serves as the backdrop for the book]?<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>McGinty:</strong> The biggest business pivot that has happened is the climate situation has gotten so bad with wind and rain — these events called surges — that the companies have started saying ‘Our cars are going to be safer than humans driving out in this weather.’<br/>\n<br/>\nBut I’m just sort of using driverless cars as a work environment to think about some of these issues [climate change, housing, capitalism].  <br/>\n<br/>\nAnd for the most part the tech actually works pretty decently in the novel. Like, infrastructure is a much bigger problem, and I don’t want to spoil anything but most of the really bad sh— that happens in the book is because of infrastructure.<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>PA Local: </strong>How many <a href=\"https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/cargasm\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">cargasms</a> are there? <br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>McGinty:</strong> You could say the whole thing is a cargasm. There’s certainly a lot of driving in it. And I welcome anyone I’ve <a href=\"https://thebaffler.com/salvos/driving-into-the-wreck-mcginty\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">made fun of</a> in the past for their writing about driving to, you know, mercilessly dunk on me, although I think I&#39;m pretty measured.<br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>PA Local:</strong> You helped develop a national <a href=\"https://pavecampaign.org/pave-presents-patrick-mcgintys-curriculum-for-an-av-centered-english-seminar/\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">college-level curriculum</a> that&#39;s all about looking at autonomous vehicles more holistically. What else prepared you to write this book? <br/>\n<br/>\n<strong>McGinty:</strong> I&#39;ve written about tech almost exclusively for two or three years.<br/>\n<br/>\nI’ve never done [autonomous vehicle] test driving work but there is a whole lot of this book that’s been influenced by my 10 years of experience as an adjunct professor at the absolute bottom — doing contract work, going on strike while my wife was eight-and-a-half months pregnant and losing our health insurance for a few days. <br/>\n<br/>\nI’ve also done a lot of reading about driverless vehicles and a lot of observing of driverless vehicles.<br/>\n<br/>\nMy grandpa was a steel worker and he was also a painter. I’ve got paintings of deer and pheasants from him all over my house. I see it. I’m sitting in front of it. My students are talking about NFTs.
