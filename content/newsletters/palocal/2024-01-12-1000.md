The year was 1972 and the art historians on hand pointed out a notable lack of female representation at a recent Corcoran Biennial — one of the longest-running showcases of American art. <br/>\n<br/>\n“As a result of that meeting, my awareness of feminism was piqued,” she said.<br/>\n<br/>\nBurko, a prominent artist in Philadelphia, wanted to address that problem in her hometown. The celebration will feature a reprisal of the 1974 show in addition to newer elements showcasing the evolution of women-identified artists and artists of color around issues like social justice, race, and gender identities.<br/>\n<br/>\nBurko said many artists from the original 1974 show will participate in this year’s event.<br/>\n<br/>\n“I am very encouraged and gratified that we are not the only ones who think it is important to bring these issues up again,” she added.<br/>\n<br/>\nThis year’s event will feature over 300 artists total at venues citywide.<br/>\n<br/>\n“The fight for a feminist future continues in 2024 and (re)FOCUS provides that platform to awaken the interests of women, especially, and all who fight for equality to have their voices heard,” said Marsha Moss, a professional public art curator and one of the co-directors for (re)FOCUS.<br/>\n<br/>\nJulie Linowes, a multimedia artist with decades of experience, will showcase her exhibit, <em>Leave Your Tie at the Door</em>, which explores issues like the overturning of <em>Roe v. Wade</em>.<br/>\n<br/>\nHer art heavily focused on her personal experiences up until 2015 when she started leaning more into politics in her art. <br/>\n<br/>\n“I felt so preoccupied with what was going on in this country and what was going on in the world that I felt like I wanted to be a part of this convo. That is what motivated me to start thinking about our political reality and incorporating my feelings into my work,” she added.<br/>\n<br/>\nHer exhibit will have suspended artworks and different videos showing women marching in protest across several decades.<br/>\n<br/>\n“I feel privileged to be asked.
