precipitation that started one-and-a-half centuries ago with a farmer mentioning, in passing, that it always seemed to <a href=\"https://raindayfestival.com/history/\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">rain on his birthday</a> (July 29). That observation inspired an annual tradition that has morphed into something big.<br/>\n<br/>\nThere’s <a href=\"https://www.facebook.com/RainDayFestival\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">a festival</a> with umbrella contests, a mascot named “Wayne Drop,” pageants, races, and an annual rain wager that has drawn bets from some of the most famous people in the world: Muhammad Ali, Will Ferrell, a pre-presidency Donald Trump, Bing Crosby, The Three Stooges, Johnny Carson, and the prince of Pennsylvania holiday quirkdom, Punxsutawney Phil. (<a href=\"https://raindayfestival.com/hat-bet/\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">Most of them have lost</a>.)<br/>\n<br/>\nHere’s how the wager or “hat bet” works: Since at least 1939, the borough’s mayor predicts rain on July 29, while celebrities and special guests bet against it. If it rains, the mayor gets a signed cap. If it doesn’t rain, the special guest wins a Rain Day souvenir. <img align=\"right\" data-file-id=\"1955932\" height=\"233\" src=\"https://mcusercontent.com/77370ff1d001f9bb991fed9e7/images/5d86de16-88da-c281-4435-74112229aa49.jpg\" style=\"border: 0px;width: 300px;height: 233px;margin: 15px 0px 15px 15px;outline: none;text-decoration: none;-ms-interpolation-mode: bicubic;\" width=\"300\"/><br/>\n<br/>\nAccording to a brief history of the holiday written by Waynesburg’s Special Events Commission chair, John Owen, the hat bet started between the early keepers of the holiday and grew.<br/>\n<br/>\nHarry Anderson of <em>Night Court </em>fame won his bet in 1988 and displayed the hat he was awarded on the set of the show, Owen wrote. It reportedly appeared in several episodes on a bookshelf in Judge Harry T. Stone’s chambers, near the stuffed armadillo.<br/>\n<br/>\nTo attract notable figures, the mayor of Waynesburg has <a href=\"https://observer-reporter.com/news/localnews/rain-day-tradition-continues-with-annual-hat-bet/article_abfbafa8-db01-5dc8-a748-bb14af94f553.html\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">relied on local connections</a>, the Observer-Reporter explained. In 2014, then-Mayor ​​Duncan Berryman reached actress <a href=\"https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0005004/\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">Patricia Heaton</a> through a friend of a friend, Waynesburg native and <em>Battlestar Galactica</em> alum <a href=\"https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0750706/?ref_=fn_al_nm_1\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">Sarah Rush</a>.<br/>\n<br/>\nIn 1983, <em>Today Show</em> weatherman Willard Scott gave Rain Day a shoutout on the national stage. In 1985, he served as hat bettor and won. <br/>\n<br/>\nThis year’s bettor is Pittsburgh gadabout Rick Sebak, and chances are he’s going to lose: Myranda Fullerton, a meteorologist with the National Weather Service in Pittsburgh, said there was a roughly 70% chance of rain in Waynesburg this Rain Day.<br/>\n<br/>\nA confident Sebak told the Observer-Reporter last week that he has every intention of driving to Waynesburg on Rain Day <a href=\"https://observer-reporter.com/news/localnews/rick-sebak-is-making-the-hat-bet-for-this-years-rain-day/article_df575bf2-013f-11ed-bf01-0ba779ad7e68.html\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">with the top down</a>.<br/>\n<br/>\nBut the historical odds aren’t in his favor either.<br/>\n<br/>\nIt has rained on 116 out of the past 148 Rain Days (or roughly 78% of the time) going all the way back to 1874, proving that the farmer who started it all, Caleb Ely, may have been onto something when he walked into William Allison’s drug store and noted his soggy birthday streak.<br/>\n<br/>\nAllison started the annual record of July 29 weather, and his project has <a href=\"https://raindayfestival.com/rain-record/\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">continued ever since</a>, spanning generations and the occasional drought.<br/>\n<br/>\nThe first dry Rain Day occurred in 1921 after 47 straight years of rain. <br/>\n<br/>\nThere have been dry spells, too, the longest lasting four years, a low-water mark that would have been matched last year had the skies not opened up.<br/>\n<br/>\nSome years see no rain. Not a shower. Not a sprinkle. Not a drop.
