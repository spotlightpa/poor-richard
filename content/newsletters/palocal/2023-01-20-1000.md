Lived here since the day I was born and did not know that,” <a href=\"https://twitter.com/idealskeptic/status/1615505900135145472\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">@idealskeptic tweeted</a>. <br/>\n<br/>\nThat sense of surprise was shared by plenty of others, while the song choice itself was both <a href=\"https://twitter.com/RyanDeto/status/1615418280709181440\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">challenged</a> and <a href=\"https://twitter.com/EzraThrush/status/1615533885064110086\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">defended</a>. <br/>\n<br/>\nWe’ve been here before, both on <a href=\"https://www.pennlive.com/entertainment/2015/01/pennsylvania_has_a_state_song.html\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">prior </a><a href=\"https://www.pennlive.com/entertainment/2015/01/pennsylvania_has_a_state_song.html\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">inauguration days</a> and decades ago when the push to adopt a state song really hit its stride in Harrisburg, unleashing lots of strong feelings about what it should and shouldn’t be, and whether one was even needed.<br/>\n<br/>\nFormer state Rep. James Gallen (R., Berks) certainly thought it was, writing his own ditty to that effect: “Without a song, no way to celebrate,” <a href=\"https://twitter.com/sayer_of_stuff/status/1383665076314411009\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">he offered</a>. “Without a song, Pa. is out of date.”<br/>\n<br/>\nPennsylvania&#39;s Historical and Museum Commission says suggestions included Billy Joel’s “Allentown” — a song Allentown itself <a href=\"https://www.wfmz.com/entertainment/allentown-to-rewrite-lyrics-of-billy-joels-hit-song/article_59c48f68-7fc7-571b-acf1-ceae23a3f17c.html\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">learned to hate</a>; “Philadelphia Freedom” by Elton John; “Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania” by Guy Mitchell; “Camptown Races” by Pittsburgh’s own Stephen Foster; and the venerable “Pennsylvania Polka.” <br/>\n<br/>\nBut Pennsylvania lawmakers went in a different direction and landed on an original composition with more copyright flexibility.<br/>\n<br/>\nIt’s called “<a href=\"https://www.wtae.com/article/pennsylvania-inauguration-josh-shapiro-governor-pittsburgh-youth-chorus/42538452\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">Pennsylvania</a>,” and it was written by Ronnie Bonner and Eddie Khoury and formally adopted by the state in 1990, beating out runners-up like &#34;<a href=\"https://www.legis.state.pa.us/CFDOCS/Legis/PN/Public/btCheck.cfm?txtType=PDF&amp;sessYr=1989&amp;sessInd=0&amp;billBody=S&amp;billTyp=B&amp;billNbr=0180&amp;pn=0180\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">Pennsylvania - Gee! It&#39;s Great!</a>&#34; and several other tunes also named “Pennsylvania,” a word that rhymes with almost nothing. <br/>\n<br/>\nEddie Khoury, a retired administrator with the Philadelphia prison system, told the AP (<a href=\"https://www.newspapers.com/image/483342044/?terms=eddie%20khoury&amp;match=1\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">paywall</a>) in 1989: &#34;When you throw 200 to 300 songs in the pot and yours comes out on top, you&#39;ve got to have an element of luck.&#34;<br/>\n<br/>\n&#34;Pennsylvania&#34; by Khoury and Bonner — a longtime Philadelphia-area songwriting team — was written decades earlier and recommended to the legislature by a committee of music educators and professionals. <br/>\n<br/>\nFor years, lawmakers had offered competing song options, most playing to their regional constituencies. State Rep. Merle Phillips (R., Northumberland) was among the lawmakers to vote against &#34;Pennsylvania,&#34; saying at the time (<a href=\"https://www.newspapers.com/image/483342044/?terms=merle&amp;match=1\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">paywall</a>), &#34;I had introduced a song ... that I thought was nicer.&#34;<br/>\n<br/>\nState Rep. Gerard Kosinski (D., Philadelphia) added: &#34;In my mind, the only state song is &#39;Pennsylvania Polka.&#39;&#34; <br/>\n<br/>\nStill, the backers of &#34;Pennsylvania&#34; won out and this state became the 49th out of 50 to adopt an official jam, leaving only New Jersey without one. <br/>\n<br/>\nA Spotlight PA <a href=\"https://twitter.com/StephenJ_Caruso/status/1615402094910898207\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">colleague said</a> this week that the winning “Pennsylvania” — a no-frills, two verse-two chorus anthem — sounds like “I’ve Been Working on the Railroad,” and now I can’t unhear it. (<a href=\"https://www.wtae.com/article/pennsylvania-inauguration-josh-shapiro-governor-pittsburgh-youth-chorus/42538452\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">Y</a><a href=\"https://www.wtae.com/article/pennsylvania-inauguration-josh-shapiro-governor-pittsburgh-youth-chorus/42538452\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">ou decide</a>.)<br/>\n<br/>\nBut it’s got more college fight song to it and lots more references to <a href=\"https://www.legis.state.pa.us/WU01/LI/LI/US/PDF/1990/0/0150..PDF\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">vanquishing tyranny</a> and proclaiming freedom.<br/>\n<br/>\nA recurring criticism of the song is that it&#39;s slightly stodgy compared to some of the others nationwide: &#34;Georgia on My Mind&#34; in, you guessed it, Georgia, and Rodgers and Hammerstein&#39;s &#34;Oklahoma,&#34; to name a few. Some states even have official rock and folk songs.<br/>\n<br/>\n&#34;Pennsylvania&#34; is neither. Here’s the hook. Everybody now.<br/>\n<br/>\n<em>“Pennsylvania, Pennsylvania,               <br/>\nMay your future be,            <br/>\nfilled with honor everlasting                <br/>\nas your history.” </em><br/>\n<br/>\nBut nothing says there can only be one state song. West Virginia has no fewer than four, “Take Me Home, Country Roads” among them.<br/>\n<br/>\nThe issue then maybe isn&#39;t whether Pennsylvania picked the right or wrong state song but rather why it didn&#39;t pick more of them. It&#39;s not too late.<br/>\n<br/>\nIf lawmakers are to ever consider giving “Pennsylvania” some company, they could pick a &#34;state polka song,&#34; a &#34;state rock song,&#34; a &#34;state waltz,&#34; anything really. May I recommend another “Pennsylvania,” this one performed by South African singer-songwriter <a href=\"https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ne3s252CIks\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #5d9eca;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">Matthew Mole</a>. <br/>\n<br/>\nWhat songs do you think should be in Pennsylvania’s official rotation?
