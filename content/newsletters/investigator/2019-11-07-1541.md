left;\"><em>By Aneri Pattani</em></p>\n\n<p style=\"font-family: Arial, &#34;Helvetica Neue&#34;, Helvetica, sans-serif;line-height: 150%;margin: 10px 0;padding: 0;mso-line-height-rule: href=\"https://www.spotlightpa.org/news/2019/10/pa.s-largest-community-college-eliminates-campus-mental-health-counseling-for-17k-students/\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: HACC, Central Pennsylvania’s Community College, made the change without any public announcement to students. We first learned about it through a tip.</p>\n\n<p style=\"font-family: Arial, &#34;Helvetica Neue&#34;, Helvetica, sans-serif;line-height: 150%;margin: 10px 0;padding: 0;mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #444444;font-size: 16px;text-align: left;\">Here’s why we’re continuing to follow the story:</p>\n\n<p dir=\"ltr\" style=\"font-family: Arial, &#34;Helvetica Neue&#34;, Helvetica, sans-serif;line-height: 150%;margin: 10px 0;padding: 0;mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #444444;font-size: 16px;text-align: left;\"><strong>Refresh my memory: What’s happening at HACC?</strong></p>\n\n<p dir=\"ltr\" style=\"font-family: Arial, &#34;Helvetica Neue&#34;, Helvetica, sans-serif;line-height: 150%;margin: 10px 0;padding: 0;mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #444444;font-size: 16px;text-align: left;\">HACC, which has five campuses across Central Pennsylvania, told counselors in September to stop providing mental health counseling to students. Instead, students are to be sent to deans of student affairs, who will then refer them to an off-campus provider. College health experts called the move risky given the rising demand for campus mental health services. Also, on-campus programs are typically free, while off-campus help can be expensive for those who don&#39;t have good insurance or any insurance at all.</p>\n\n<p dir=\"ltr\" style=\"font-family: Arial, &#34;Helvetica Neue&#34;, Helvetica, sans-serif;line-height: 150%;margin: 10px 0;padding: 0;mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #444444;font-size: 16px;text-align: left;\"><strong>Why does it matter if HACC offers mental health services?</strong></p>\n\n<p dir=\"ltr\" style=\"font-family: Arial, &#34;Helvetica Neue&#34;, Helvetica, sans-serif;line-height: 150%;margin: 10px 0;padding: 0;mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #444444;font-size: 16px;text-align: left;\">Over the past decade, there’s been a persistent rise in the number of college students nationwide <a href=\"https://sites.psu.edu/ccmh/files/2019/01/2018-Annual-Report-1.30.19-ziytkb.pdf\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #009edb;font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;\">experiencing depression, anxiety and suicidal thoughts</a>. Researchers have found that by keeping students in school and paying tuition, colleges can more than <a href=\"https://www.acenet.edu/Documents/Investing-in-Student-Mental-Health.pdf\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #009edb;font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;\">recoup the cost of mental health services.</a></p>\n\n<p dir=\"ltr\" style=\"font-family: Arial, &#34;Helvetica Neue&#34;, Helvetica, sans-serif;line-height: 150%;margin: 10px 0;padding: 0;mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #444444;font-size: 16px;text-align: left;\"><strong>How does HACC’s decision compare to what other colleges are doing?</strong></p>\n\n<p dir=\"ltr\" style=\"font-family: Arial, &#34;Helvetica Neue&#34;, Helvetica, sans-serif;line-height: 150%;margin: 10px 0;padding: 0;mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #444444;font-size: 16px;text-align: left;\">Many colleges across the country are trying to increase their mental health services for students. Just last month, Stanford University <a href=\"https://www.insidehighered.com/news/2019/10/08/stanford-changes-leave-policies-mental-illness\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #009edb;font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;\">agreed to rewrite its involuntary leave of absence policy</a> after students filed a class action lawsuit claiming the college was forcing students with mental illness off campus. Closer to home, the University of Pennsylvania has hired a chief wellness officer and several counselors in response to 14 student suicides since 2013.</p>\n\n<p dir=\"ltr\" style=\"font-family: Arial, &#34;Helvetica Neue&#34;, Helvetica, sans-serif;line-height: 150%;margin: 10px 0;padding: 0;mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #444444;font-size: 16px;text-align: left;\"><strong>What other issues around college mental health are you investigating?</strong></p>\nIn addition to being a Spotlight PA reporter, I’m currently <a href=\"https://www.cartercenter.org/news/pr/2019/mhj-fellows-2019-2020.html\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #009edb;font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;\">a Rosalynn Carter Mental Health Journalism Fellow</a>.
