Pennsylvania legislature spent an average of $51 million a year on food, lodging, travel, and other perks from 2017-2020, but you&#39;d never know it.<br/>\n<br/>\nThat&#39;s according to a new, year-long, and ongoing investigation by Spotlight PA in collaboration with The Caucus, <a href=\"https://www.spotlightpa.org/news/2021/05/pa-legislature-expense-accounts-hidden-legislative-privilege/\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: spending.</a><br/>\n<br/>\nThe project involved dozens of public records requests, an analysis of 400,000 legislative expenses, and pushback from legislative lawyers — all to figure out what should be a simple question: How are lawmakers spending your money?<br/>\n<br/>\nIn total, from 2017 to 2020, the legislature spent $203 million <a href=\"https://www.spotlightpa.org/news/2021/05/pa-legislature-expense-accounts-hidden-legislative-privilege/\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #009edb;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">to feed, house, transport, and provide rental offices and other perks for lawmakers and their staffs</a>.<br/>\n<br/>\nAbout one in 10 of those dollars — $20 million in all over the four years — went into lawmakers&#39; pockets in the form of reimbursements for meals, mileage subsidies, per diems (<a href=\"https://www.spotlightpa.org/news/2021/03/pa-coronavirus-lawmakers-legislature-expenses-highest-paid-united-states/\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #009edb;font-weight: bold;text-decoration: underline;\">more on those here</a>), and other expenses.<br/>\n<br/>\nThat&#39;s on top of salaries that are already among the highest of any legislature in the nation, dinners on the dime of lobbyists and industry groups, and access to campaign war chests that some have used for everything from a new pair of sneakers to a jaunt through Europe.<br/>\n<br/>\nMore than a decade ago, a grand jury investigating state lawmakers&#39; misuse of public money recommended the General Assembly make dramatic changes to how it conducted its business — including changes to its spending practices.<br/>\n<br/>\nThe grand jurors recommended eliminating taxpayer-funded partisan caucuses, requiring receipts for lodging and food reimbursements, and reducing the number of district offices to one per legislator. It also recommended that the legislature&#39;s budget itemize spending, rather than lumping expenditures under broad categories that mean little to the general public.<br/>\n<br/>\nNone of those changes were implemented. Though some lawmakers pledged increased transparency of their expenses, another Spotlight PA/Caucus review out today finds <a href=\"https://www.spotlightpa.org/news/2021/05/pa-lawmaker-expenses-transparency-websites/\" target=\"_blank\" style=\"mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust:
