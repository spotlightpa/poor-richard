From dive bars and diners to town halls and bait shops, Jolene McIlwain’s <em>Sidle Creek</em> paints an intimate picture of western Pennsylvania’s Appalachian hills. The short story collection “interrogates the myths and stereotypes” of rural, farming towns where McIlwain grew up. Each story tackles life in the “mine-bruised,” working-class towns and carries themes of grief, environment, and community care.
