HARRISBURG — Twenty Pennsylvania legislators were allegedly on the “hit list” of a Lebanon County man who made online threats that included a picture of a firearm and referenced a “Memorial Day Operation,” law enforcement records show.

The man, Adam G. Berryhill, was arrested by Pennsylvania State Police officials last week and charged with making terroristic threats, according to an agency incident report. The report states that Berryhill allegedly posted “politically violent” posts on his X account in late April and made statements about “shooting.”

The State Police report lists 20 state lawmakers — all Democrats — from the House and Senate who were allegedly threatened. They include House Speaker Joanna McClinton, Sen. Sharif Street, and Reps. Jordan Harris and Chris Rabb.

At least one of those lawmakers said she only learned of the threats on Tuesday. State Sen. Katie Muth, a Democrat from Montgomery County, said she learned of the arrest from a colleague who had received a letter in the mail on Tuesday stating that Berryhill is scheduled for a court hearing this week. (Crime victims are alerted to such proceedings).

“I think it’s egregious,” said Muth, who searched her office mail on Tuesday and also found a notice. “There is an utter disregard for our existence. … This is wild to me.”

Reached for comment, State Police spokesperson Logan Brouse said the legislature’s sergeant at arms is responsible for notifying lawmakers of threats.

Brouse could not immediately say when or whether State Police had informed the sergeant of arms about the arrest.

Legislative leadership did not immediately provide comment, though one lawmaker said a statement is forthcoming.

Berryhill is scheduled for a preliminary hearing on Thursday before a magistrate judge in Lebanon County. Online court records do not list an attorney.
